# Content Rules

## Primary Content Principles
- Search should be the primary wayfinding mechanism
- Help articles are the primary answer object
- Support is fallback, not primary navigation
- Product hubs are canonical browse destinations for products
- Role modifies discovery and relevance; it does not create separate site architecture

## Homepage Rules
- Homepage search is unified
- Do not use a homepage content-type dropdown such as Articles / FAQs / Guides
- Homepage may include shared help domains, common problems, browse by role, and browse by product
- Browse by role should route to filtered search/discovery, not role hubs
- Browse by product should route to product hubs

## Search Results Rules
- Results should be article-first
- Filters may include role, product, sign-in path, education segment, and last updated
- Content-type-first browsing should not drive the experience

## Sign-In and Account Rules
- Sign In & Account Help is a shared help domain
- Common account issues should appear before less common sign-in paths
- Reset-password should be modeled as routing logic rather than a single flat help article
- Direct Cengage account password reset should hand off externally
- LMS and school-managed password paths should provide instructions and routing help

## Product Hub Rules
- Product hubs are canonical
- Product hubs are product-first and role-aware
- Do not create separate role-first product sites

## Help Article Rules
- Articles should include content, metadata, related help, and helpfulness feedback
- Metadata should support search filtering and related-content logic

## Support Rules
- Support should be presented after self-service options
- Do not assume universal phone support prominence
- Do not assume submit-a-case is always the primary support action

## MVP Constraints
- Existing content authoring remains in Oxygen/DITA
- HTML output is consumed by the prototype/new front end
- Large-scale content restructuring is out of scope for Phase 1
